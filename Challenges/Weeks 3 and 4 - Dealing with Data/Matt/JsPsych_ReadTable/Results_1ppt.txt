		    				The aim of this research was to investigate the speed at which humans estimate the distance of objects/surfaces in real-world scenes. We manipulated image viewing time in order to determine the speed at which humans (i) judge which is farther/closer, and (ii) judge the relative distance of two locations. We also varied whether one of the locations was higher in the image than the other, to look at whether humans use height as a source of information for judging depth. Your participation in this research will help inform current understandings of the time-course of depth-estimation in real-world scenes. We know that an understanding of the relative depth of objects/surfaces is critical for a number of tasks including interacting with objects on our desk, judging walking distances, and navigating through real-world environments.
